Privacy Policy

User Data

Fuel does not store, copy, or transfer any user data except 1) as necessary to save foods, meals, and other user entries to a user's private iCloud database and on device, and 2) to upload, with permission, foods created for use by other Fuel users via Fuel's food search. Fuel connects via Apple provided authentication mechanisms to transfer data to your private iCloud account.

We firmly believe in privacy and will never utilize tracking cookies or other user-hostile technologies that degrade the user experience.

Diagnostic and Usage Data

Fuel does not collect or receive any diagnostic or usage data beyond what is provided by Apple as part of our participation in the Apple Developer Program. This diagnostic and usage data includes, but is not limited to, the iOS operating system version, device type, Fuel app version, and any applicable crash logs. Sending diagnostic and usage data to Apple can be enabled or disabled within iOS from the Settings app.

Third-Parties

Fuel transfers your food data to the iCloud service maintained and operated by Apple and connected to your Apple ID.

Fuel integrates with two third-party partners: 1) We use RevenueCat to request information about in-app purchases using Apple-provided methods. We do not store or collect any identifying information using these requests. User identities generated within Fuel for RevenueCat are anonymous. 2) Fuel uses Typesense for search results. Typesense does not store, copy, or transfer any user data. The integration with the search service is purposely dumb and only returns truncated versions of food data in response to a user query.

Website

The Fuel app website located at getfuelapp.com integrates with Google Analytics to track industry-standard metrics. These metrics include but are not limited to referrals, visit duration, and visitor origin.

The Fuel website does not use tracking cookies beyond those provided by Google Analytics.
