Frequently asked questions

  • What are universal foods?

    Universal items are unbranded foods that have the same nutritional value regardless of their source. You can trust universal foods to have complete nutrient profiles. Use universal foods like peanut butter, fruits, and cuts of meat in place of branded items.

  • What’s the priority for store-brand foods?

    Many store brands are copies of the branded equivalent. Fuel prioritizes the branded version to capture as wide a variety of food stuffs as possible.

    Regional limits make it difficult for me as a solo team to scan and import foods. Expanding my food library to include certain store brands is part of my long-term efforts to build the world’s best food library.

  • How often do foods get updated?

    Hundreds of new foods are added weekly. Existing foods are checked and updated annually, or sooner if a manufacturer makes a major change. User submissions are approved on a bi-weekly schedule.

  • How can I request a food or brand?

    Feel free to reach out to me for any requests. Feedback is helpful for helping me prioritize food items for the wider community.

  • How does Fuel calculate calorie needs?

    Your calorie goal is based on how much energy your body needs at rest, also called your basal metabilic rate (BMR).

    Fuel calculates your BMR using your age, sex, and height. This number is then multiplied based on your activity level and personal goals.

    Fuel compares your calorie target to your weight trend every few weeks. You can also connect Apple Health for more accurate measurements.
