A modern, comprehensive food library

  • Universal foods No need to look for the brand. Universal foods have complete nutrient details and have similar calorie counts across different brand names.
  • Auto-sync Sync with the food library each week to ensure the most up-to-date nutrition information. Updates apply retroactively to previous days.
  • Verified foods only All user submissions go through verification to ensure search results stay meaningful. Missing data is added to ensure all foods are complete.
  • Complete nutrient profiles When possible nutrition details include nutrients beyond most labels. These items include selenium, copper, and other vitamins & minerals necessary for general health.

Frequently asked questions

  • How does Fuel calculate calorie needs?

    Your calorie goal is based on how much energy your body needs at rest, also called your basal metabolic rate (BMR). Fuel uses your age, sex, and height to calculate your BMR with the Mifflin-St Jeor formula. This number is then multiplied based on your activity level and personal goals.

  • Where does the universal food information come from?

    Universal food data is sourced from the United States Department of Agriculture and was analyzed by qualified laboratories. In some cases, the information has been merged with other sources for a more complete profile. Allergen information may also be included.

  • How often do foods get updated?

    Hundreds of new foods are added weekly. Existing foods are checked and updated annually, or sooner if a manufacturer makes a major change. User submissions are approved on a bi-weekly schedule.

  • How can I request a food or brand?

    Feel free to reach out to me for any requests. Feedback helps me prioritize food items for the wider community.
