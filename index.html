Frequently asked questions

  • What are universal foods?

    Skip the brand name! Universal items have similar ingredients between brands. This includes foods like greek yogurt, produce, and other staples like peanut butter. Choose universal foods for vitamin and mineral details that go beyond any label.

  • What’s the priority for store-brand foods?

    Many store brands are copies of the branded equivalent. Fuel prioritizes the branded version to capture as wide a variety of food stuffs as possible.

    Regional limits make it difficult for me as a solo team to scan and import foods. Expanding my food library to include certain store brands is part of my long-term efforts to build the world’s best food library.

  • How often do foods get updated?

    Hundreds of new foods are added weekly. Existing foods are checked and updated annually, or sooner if a manufacturer makes a major change. User submissions are approved on a bi-weekly schedule.

  • How can I request a food or brand?

    Feel free to reach out to me for any requests. Feedback helps me prioritize food items for the wider community.

  • How does Fuel calculate calorie needs?

    Your calorie goal is based on how much energy your body needs at rest, also called your basal metabolic rate (BMR). Fuel uses your age, sex, and height to calculate your BMR with the Mifflin-St Jeor formula. This number is then multiplied based on your activity level and personal goals.
